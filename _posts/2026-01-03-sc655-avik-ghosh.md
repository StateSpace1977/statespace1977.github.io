## Course Content
Measure theoretic setup for probability, Kalman Filtering, Markov Chains, Martingales, Concentration phenomenon, etc.

## Feedback on Lectures
Great, very informative

## Feedback on Assignments/Tutorials/Homework
No official assignments, but solving the problems in the notes are highly recommended.

## Feedback on Exams
Solve ALL the exercises in the notes, if not more, and you'll ace the exams. But might be difficult if taken lightly.

## Difficulty (on a scale of 1-5 with 5 being very tough)
4

## Textbooks/References
Several, a fairly extensive bibliography is provided. Bruce Hajek's and Shiryaev's books are often recommended.

## Final Takeaway
Extremely informative if you know the topics beforehand. Otherwise it might get difficult later on. If done properly, it might be one of the most useful probability courses you'll ever take.
